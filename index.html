Explore the culture, language, religion, and food of Greece.


Country

Location

Greece is located in southeastern Europe on the southern tip of the Balkan Peninsula.

Country: Greece

Capital: Athens

Map


Language

The official language in Greece is Modern Greek. Over 97% of the population speaks it.

Language Family

Greek belongs to the Hellenic language family, its own branch of Indo-European languages.

Influences

Greek stayed mostly independent but borrowed words from Latin, Turkish, Italian, and French.


Religion

The main religion in Greece is Greek Orthodox Christianity. About 81–90% of people identify as Greek Orthodox.

Historical Influences

Ancient Greek polytheism

Christianity (Greek Orthodox Church)

Islam during Ottoman rule


Culture

Traditions

Greek Easter is the most important celebration, with lamb roasts, fireworks, and large family gatherings.

Apokries (Carnival) is a festive time with costumes, parades, and parties before Lent.

Panigiria are traditional village feasts with music, dancing, and food.

Beliefs

Many people believe in the "Evil Eye" and use charms or symbols for protection.

Family Traditions

It is common to name children after their grandparents to honor family heritage.


Greek Food

Traditional Dishes

Moussaka

A layered dish with eggplant, minced meat, and béchamel sauce.

Souvlaki

Grilled meat skewers served with pita and vegetables.

Spanakopita

A spinach and feta cheese pie in crispy pastry.

Gyros

Meat cooked on a vertical rotisserie served in pita bread.

Tzatziki

A refreshing yogurt dip with cucumber, garlic, and herbs.

Famous Drinks

Metaxa

A Greek brandy blended with wine and herbs.

Frappe

A cold foamy iced coffee popular in summer.

Tsipouro

A strong grape-based alcoholic drink.
